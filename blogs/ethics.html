Ethics and values

Every now and then it helps to write down what you aim for, your values and what truly matters to you. And if you do this publicly, it's much easier for others to hold you accountable for it. Here I'll be writing about 4 of my core values and about the things I truly believe matter to me.

Minimalism

It's not that many years ago that I dreamt of having lots of money. Luxury watches, luxury suits, expensive and stylishly looking clothes. Nowadays I don't think I care about it.

To me, minimalism isn't the boring "I don't want any decorations on my walls" or "I'm not gonna put any stickers on my laptop" sort of living. It's more about understanding that I don't need everything to feel fulfilled.

Where the money, clothing and exterior before gave me meaning I've now come to realise that perhaps it's more of what I have on the inside- my values, my ideals, my morals- that truly matter.

I don't want a big mansion with lots of cars- I'd probably do just fine with a small flat. I don't want any fancy sports cars or big trucks- I can already get by with only public transportation. I don't need a fully stacked out gaming PC- my ThinkPad L14 with and i3 already does everything I need out of it. So why should I desire more of the exterior?
